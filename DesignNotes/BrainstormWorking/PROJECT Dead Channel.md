## Concrete Decisions

- The game's identity is a mix of plate-spinning time management, hacking simulation, roster management, and limited autobattler influence.
- The primary focus for now is the run layer, not the management layer.
- Design pillars are triage over mastery, attention as resource, and indirect control.
- Movable puzzle windows and the signal timeline are part of the intended core play experience.

## Speculative Ideas

- Many additional puzzle types, split tracks, management-layer depth, and broader campaign features remain in the long-term idea pool.

## Unresolved Questions

- Final prototype puzzle roster.
- Exact fail-state behavior and runner consequence model.
- How much long-term campaign structure will survive first implementation.

## Terminology And Definitions

- Signal: any obstacle, objective, or interactable on the timeline.
- Heat: security attention and global run pressure.
- Signal Timeline: the abstract path of the runners toward their objective.

## Mechanics And Systems Details

- Runs revolve around scanning, hacking, and reprioritizing signals on an abstract five-lane timeline.
- The center lane is the critical path, while side lanes represent adjacent spaces or opportunities.
- Mission arcs can break runs into infiltration, objective, and extraction sections with different pacing.
- Heat rises over time and through interactions, then amplifies difficulty through multiple systems.

## Narrative Or Tone Details

- Tone target is gritty independent-contractor cyberpunk inspired by Shadowrun and Invisible Inc.
