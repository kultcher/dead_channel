## Concrete Decisions

- Corporations should function as both flavor packages and mechanical tilesets.
- Distinct corp identities can structure mission feel, signal composition, IC style, terminal tone, and difficulty progression.
- A burnt-out hacker mentor is a plausible tutorial/early-advisor character.

## Speculative Ideas

- Candidate corps include military, biotech, finance, anarchic-tech, cloud-infrastructure, and heavy-industry archetypes.
- Story progression could alternate fixed story missions with proc-gen contracts.
- Corp heterogeneity can be explained by legacy systems alone or strengthened with a post-corporate collapse layer.

## Unresolved Questions

- Which corporations deserve canon status now versus staying as inspiration/reference pools.
- Whether the campaign layer needs a strong player motivation beyond money and survival.

## Terminology And Definitions

- Mechanical tileset: a corp identity that changes obstacle mix, IC style, terminal language, and mission feel.

## Mechanics And Systems Details

- Distinct corp archetypes can drive obstacle density, lethality, weirdness, redundancy, and terminal voice.
- Corp identity is a practical way to vary proc-gen missions without making every facility random noise.

## Narrative Or Tone Details

- The mentor concept adds a grounded personal voice to onboarding.
- Corp identities can help the world feel like competing institutions rather than one generic cyberpunk backdrop.

## Implementation Constraints

- Use corp identities as a modular flavor-and-mechanics framework rather than building a huge lore burden up front.

## Contradictions To Log

- Some corp differentiation can be explained by straight cyberpunk "legacy system" logic, while other passages lean toward stronger post-corporate decay.

## Merge Recommendations

- Merge the idea that corporations should each have distinct cultural and mechanical profiles.
- Keep specific corp rosters provisional.
