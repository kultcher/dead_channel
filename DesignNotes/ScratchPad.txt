Common jargon:
"The paperclip" - general reference to the AI's original purpose as a paperclip maximizer, also used coloquially to refer to hyper-fixating on a single goal or task to the detriment of all else
"drift" used colloquially to describe the world's slow decay
"patching", "patcher" - physical bypasses of old AI systems, or the people who make them
"shim" - a small patch or bypass, often temporary, to get around a system's limitations or failures
"fallback"
"downstack vs upstack" - new class signifiers
"tell it a story" - cargo-cultish behavior of manipulating stale AI systems
"baseliners" - the current generation born after genetic engineering and AI dependence
"Preserves" - aka "Jars" the old generation who have been kept alive through anti-aging tech, often in "clinics" or social clubs for the aged. They are the keepers of knowledge about the legacy systems and the world before the collapse, but are often dismissed or ignored by the younger generations.
"the catch-up" and "spilling": the rapid decline of preserves who lose access to anti-aging tech, "spilling" is the the uglier slang term
"clinic" - slang for a social club for the aged who still use anti-aging tech
"the clock" - general term people use for impending collapse when the AI systems fully fail
"seeing the depth" - the moment of realization of how little people understand the legacy systems
"Orphan/Daemon AIs"
"coasting" - the general vibe
"Husks" - abandoned AI facilities
